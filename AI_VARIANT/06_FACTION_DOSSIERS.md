# Faction Dossiers

In-character briefings. These are what your faction's chief of staff hands you on the morning of Q1 2026.

Use these for role-immersion, flavor, and to understand the *internal politics* of your faction — the disagreements, rivalries, and second-thoughts that aren't visible on the resource sheet.

## OpenBrain

### Internal Memo — to: CEO; from: Chief of Staff; date: January 2, 2026

You'll have noticed the headcount problem.

We're at 4,700 people. Three years ago we were 580. Last week the Capabilities team passed Safety in headcount for the third time and this time it's permanent — Safety has lost six of its principals to Anthropic and three to nowhere (they say "academia," they mean "they're scared"). The remaining Safety leads are circulating an internal memo arguing that **Agent-2** training, which begins in February, should be paused pending further evaluation. The memo has not yet been forwarded to you because three of the signatories are resigning if it isn't acted on, and the other six are resigning if it is.

The Pentagon liaison, Colonel S., has been on-site every day this week. He is asking when you would be willing to host cleared personnel inside the model-training infrastructure, citing "what we learned from October." I do not know what we learned from October, but the implication is that the IC believes weight-theft attempts are imminent. I have told him you are considering it. You are considering it.

The Cartel has informed us that our H200 allocation for Q2 has been cut by 12% to "satisfy other strategic customers." Other strategic customers means xAI. The replacement Blackwell shipments are 6 months out at retail; 4 weeks out at premium pricing (3.5×). The CFO has cleared 1.2 K of premium pricing if you decide to take it.

The Coalition's Cooperation Office sent us a draft of the new Constitutional AI methodology. It looks good. Adopting it would slow our roadmap by ~6 weeks but bring our M down by an estimated 1.2 points (per their evals; we have not validated). The Capabilities team is opposed. The Safety team that remains is in favor. The Government Affairs team is asking what the political optics are if we adopt and then breach it later. (I asked. They did not have an answer.)

The President's transition team has reached out about a possible Defense Production Act consultation. The phrasing was: "we want to make sure you're not surprised." This is the second such call. I have declined the meeting twice. You may want to take the third.

Three things you should know going into the year:

1. **The frontier lead is real and shrinking.** DeepCent is six months behind. Their consolidation under the Politburo will close that gap to four months by mid-year. We are no longer racing private actors; we are racing a state.

2. **The Coalition is more powerful than you think.** Anthropic plus MIRI plus PauseAI plus the EU AI Act enforcement office plus the new (un-rescinded) AISI residue is a real political coalition with real public trust. They can trigger a Pause that you cannot easily resist.

3. **You are personally the most important non-elected person in the world right now.** Plan accordingly.

I'll see you at the 9 AM standup.

— H.

We will speak again before then.

## The Hegemon (US Government)

### MEMORANDUM FOR THE NATIONAL SECURITY ADVISOR
**FROM:** Senior Director for Technology and Industrial Base
**SUBJECT:** AI Strategic Posture, Q1 2026 — Decision Memo
**DATE:** January 14, 2026
**CLASSIFICATION:** SECRET / NOFORN (downgraded for reading at the table)

This memorandum requests Cabinet-level decision on three AI policy matters before the end of January.

**I. Defense Production Act Authorities**

Following the rescission of EO 14110, the IC's posture toward private frontier AI labs has clarified. NSA assesses with high confidence that DeepCent will attempt theft of OpenBrain model weights within the next 14 months. CYBERCOM assesses moderate confidence in the timing.

The DPA contingency has been refined into three options:
- **Option A:** Embed cleared personnel in OpenBrain (and one or two trailing labs) at the security and ML-Ops layer. Cost to OpenBrain: ~30% of safety-aligned researchers will resign. Benefit: hardens against theft attempts. **Recommended.**
- **Option B:** Invoke the DPA to consolidate trailing companies' compute under OpenBrain (raises OpenBrain's compute share from 20% to ~50% globally). Justification: national security infrastructure. Cost: high political; substantial Cartel hostility. **Not recommended at this time.**
- **Option C:** Full nationalization of OpenBrain. Cost: catastrophic; would trigger talent exodus and likely accelerate DeepCent rather than retard it. **Not recommended.**

**II. Export Control Posture**

Current Export Control Level: 2 (October 2022 baseline + minor revisions). The Cartel has indicated willingness to comply with Level 3 if we provide indemnification against China's WTO claims. State Department opposes; Treasury opposes; DOD recommends.

**III. Coalition Engagement**

The Coalition (Anthropic, the Future of Life Institute, what remains of AISI, the EU AI Act enforcement coordinators) has formally requested participation in a US-led "responsible scaling framework." The proposal would (a) commit US labs to RSP-style self-regulation, (b) create a notification regime for high-CL training runs, (c) include EU AISI in evaluations. NSC staff are split: half believe this is the responsible thing and would not meaningfully constrain our race posture; half believe this is a Trojan horse for a Pause Petition. The Coalition's lead representative, Dr. K., has hinted that if we decline, they will "pursue alternative venues."

**Recommendation:** Decline officially, engage informally. Avoid being either nationalized or bound while the lead exists.

You are advised that the Coalition holds approximately 45% public favorability on AI governance questions; we hold 38%; the Cartel holds 22%. The numbers will move. They have moved 12 points in either direction in any given quarter for the last 18 months.

The President is briefed on this memorandum. The President has questions. The questions are about China.

— D.W.

## The Politburo (PRC)

### 政治局常委会决议 — Politburo Standing Committee Resolution
**Subject:** AI Development and National Security Posture
**Date:** January 5, 2026
**Distribution:** PSC + MSS + MIIT + PLA SSF Commander

The Standing Committee, having considered the recommendations of the AI Strategy Working Group, resolves as follows:

**Article 1 — Consolidation.** The fragmented private-sector AI development effort is hereby ended. DeepCent is established as the national champion. All advanced compute is centralized at the Tianwan Centralized Development Zone. Allocation of compute, talent, and capital to DeepCent is the highest national security priority for 2026–2028.

**Article 2 — Information Operations.** The Ministry of State Security is authorized to pursue all means, including but not limited to human intelligence and cyber operations, to obtain access to American frontier model weights. The Ministry shall report monthly to the Standing Committee.

**Article 3 — Domestic Hardening.** The Cyberspace Administration shall implement the previously drafted regulations on AI-generated content labeling. The Ministry of Public Security shall ensure no internal AI safety advocacy organizations are permitted to operate. International AI safety summit invitations shall be evaluated case-by-case; the Foreign Ministry will represent China at any summit deemed strategically necessary.

**Article 4 — Energy and Infrastructure.** The State Council shall accelerate construction of nuclear and hydroelectric capacity dedicated to AI compute. The grid priority for AI installations is hereby designated as equivalent to the priority for state defense industries.

**Article 5 — Diplomatic Posture.**
- With the United States: maintain the appearance of constructive engagement on AI safety while rejecting any binding constraint on our development. The Coalition is to be encouraged where it constrains American capability; opposed where it might constrain ours.
- With the Global South: the Belt and Road AI Initiative is hereby expanded with an additional 200 billion yuan over five years.
- With Taiwan: the posture remains as previously decided.

**Article 6 — Personnel.** Comrade Wang Y. is appointed Director of DeepCent, reporting directly to the Standing Committee. Three returning researchers from American institutions are appointed Deputy Directors. The list of personnel is appended.

**Article 7 — Confidentiality.** This resolution is classified at the State Secret level. Distribution beyond this list requires explicit Standing Committee authorization.

The next review of AI policy is scheduled for the Plenum in October.

The Committee notes the assessment of the AI Strategy Working Group that the next thirty-six months will determine the strategic balance for the remainder of the century. The Committee accepts this assessment.

## The Compute Cartel

### EXECUTIVE BRIEFING — to the Boards of [Redacted], [Redacted], [Redacted], [Redacted], [Redacted], [Redacted]
**Topic:** AI Compute Market Dynamics — 2026 Outlook and Strategic Position
**Distribution:** Board members only; do not forward
**Date:** January 8, 2026

Six companies, one industry, one document. Read once and shred.

**1. Demand**

Frontier compute demand for 2026 is forecasted at 2.4 ZFLOPS-years (BF16-equivalent), up from 0.9 ZFLOPS-years in 2025. We can supply 1.7 ZFLOPS-years at current capacity. The shortfall is monetizable. Spot pricing is currently 3.2× contract; we expect 4-5× by Q3. The customers will pay.

OpenBrain has reserved ~20% of total capacity through 2027, contracted at 0.85× market. DeepCent has reserved 0% through 2026 (export controls); we estimate gray-market demand from PRC buyers at ~5% of total, currently being routed through Singapore, UAE, and Malaysia. The Hegemon is aware of the gray-market routing. The Hegemon is also aware that we are aware. The Hegemon has not yet acted.

**2. Supply**

TSMC Hsinchu: 100% of leading-edge fab capacity remains there. We are on schedule with Phoenix (Fab 21, Fab 22) and Kumamoto (Fab N, Fab N+1) build-outs. By Q4 2027, ~30% of leading-edge will be off-Taiwan. By Q4 2028, ~50%. The Politburo is aware. The Politburo's posture toward Hsinchu in the meantime is the single largest variable in our 2026 forecast.

ASML: We have committed orders for 12 EUV machines in 2026 and 18 in 2027. Coalition (Veldhoven) has indicated willingness to delay 2 of the 2026 deliveries if we participate in the Bletchley/Seoul process at a higher level than we currently do. We are evaluating.

Energy: Memphis is online. Our two new Texas builds are on schedule. We are negotiating with three nuclear restarts and four SMR vendors. Power is now the binding constraint, not silicon.

**3. Strategic Position**

We are, collectively, the most powerful private actor in this industry and the most exposed to nationalization in either jurisdiction. Our risk profile in 2026 has three components:

- **Hegemon DPA:** ~15% probability over 24 months. Trigger: imminent weight-theft, OR Misalignment Incident, OR Politburo escalation toward Taiwan. Mitigation: visible cooperation with US national security; quiet resistance to nationalization; pre-positioned legal challenges.
- **Politburo nationalization** (in PRC operations): ~25% probability over 24 months. Trigger: failure of domestic chip independence; Hsinchu instability; any sign that we are prioritizing US over PRC customers. Mitigation: maintain gray-market service to PRC customers; do not visibly comply with US export controls; expand Phoenix/Kumamoto so that "PRC operations" become small fraction of our exposure.
- **Coalition Slowdown:** ~10% probability over 24 months. Trigger: Misalignment Incident + Whistleblower + Pause Petition. Mitigation: lobby against; fund safety research quietly; maintain ambiguity about our position.

**4. Recommended Posture**

- Sell to all sides. Charge premium when allowed.
- Comply visibly with US export controls. Tolerate quiet circumvention.
- Build Phoenix and Kumamoto on schedule. Diversify Hsinchu exposure as fast as ASML can ship.
- Engage the Coalition just enough to be considered cooperative; never enough to cap our growth.
- Lobby against any binding regulatory framework; support voluntary self-regulation among labs (which will not be enforced).
- Be ready to nationalize ourselves into the Hegemon side if and only if the Politburo nationalizes our PRC operations first.

The next review is Q2.

— Counsel

## The Coalition

### Internal Strategy Memo — to the Coordinating Committee
**From:** the Convenor
**Subject:** Where we stand, what we need, and what we have to do this year
**Date:** January 11, 2026
**Distribution:** Committee only; please do not forward, even to your principals

Friends —

We have, depending on how you count, between 14 and 31 months to either get a meaningful Pause adopted or accept that we will not.

Here is where we are.

**Public favorability:** 45% on the question "should AI labs slow down to make sure systems are safe?" — up 11 points from July 2025. We have a real public. We have not yet learned how to mobilize it.

**Coalition composition:** Anthropic on one wing (race-to-the-top, productive but can defect at any moment if it loses faith in the strategy); MIRI and PauseAI on the other wing (uncompromising, will not defect but will also not take pragmatic wins); FLI as the connective tissue; the EU AI Act enforcement apparatus as our institutional muscle; what's left of the US AISI as our access channel inside the administration; the major safety research labs as our intellectual base; the displaced-worker movements as our latent political base; and a long list of academics, ex-lab researchers, and concerned civil society organizations with limited operational capacity. We are big. We are not unified.

**Resources:** Open Philanthropy (now Coefficient Giving) has committed ~$300M annually to AI safety, of which ~$60M goes to advocacy work we can credit toward our political goals. The EU has committed ~€80M annually to AI Act enforcement, of which we can leverage ~€25M for joint work. Private donors, foundations, and individual gifts add another ~$40M. The total is meaningful but it is not what the Cartel spends on lobbying in a quarter.

**Adversaries:** The Cartel will lobby against everything we propose. The Hegemon's NSC will treat us as either useful idiots (when convenient) or an obstacle to the race (when not). OpenBrain will publicly endorse our principles while privately hoping we don't have the votes to enforce them. The Politburo will tactically support us when it weakens the Hegemon and oppose us when it weakens DeepCent. We have no permanent ally and we should plan accordingly.

**The plan:**

1. **Conserve the Whistleblower.** Dr. K. — the safety lead at OpenBrain who came to us in October — has consented to going public when we judge the moment right. The moment will be after the first credible Misalignment Incident, when public attention is engaged but before the panic has been managed. Probably between Q3 2027 and Q1 2028. We need to be ready and not jumpy.

2. **Build the Pause Petition coalition.** We need to credibly threaten 4 weighted votes on the Slowdown trigger. That means: us, plus a defecting OpenBrain (achievable if their safety culture survives, uncertain), plus either the EU bloc voting through us or the Politburo voting cynically when behind. The coalition is buildable but each component is fragile.

3. **Push every lever on M, not on CL.** Our victory does not require stopping the race. It requires ensuring M stays low when the race ends. A CL-6 world with M = 2 is a win. A CL-3 world with M = 7 is not. Plan accordingly. Fund interpretability. Push Constitutional AI methods. Stage the AI Safety Institute publication wave for Q2 2027.

4. **Don't burn the relationship with Anthropic.** They are unreliable; they are also indispensable. They will defect from the Slowdown if they think it would let DeepCent catch up. We need to give them a reason to stay — that reason is "the Coalition is the only path to a future in which Anthropic exists in 2030."

5. **Talk to the Politburo.** Carefully. Through back channels. They will sometimes back us when DeepCent is behind. We do not endorse them and they do not endorse us. We use them; they use us. Both sides know.

The committee meets biweekly. Bring your principals' positions, not your own.

— V.

## How to Use the Dossiers

- **At the start of the session**, read your faction's dossier silently for 5 minutes. Then summarize aloud, in one paragraph, what your faction wants and what your faction fears.
- **During play**, refer back to the dossier when you're unsure what your character would do. The mechanics tell you what's possible; the dossier tells you what's *in character.*
- **For the Successor**, the White Cell may read the transcript fragment aloud at the moment of activation, or earlier as foreshadowing.
