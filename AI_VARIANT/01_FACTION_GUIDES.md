# Faction Guides

Per-faction playbooks. Read your own carefully; skim the others to know what they want and what they fear.

---

## OpenBrain

*Lead US private lab. The frontier is yours to lose.*

### Identity

You are the composite of the lab everyone else is trying to catch, slow down, regulate, copy, or recruit from. Your founders wrote essays about democratizing AI. Your investors expect a return. Your safety team is publishing memos about deceptive alignment. Your government contact is asking how soon you can put Agent‑3 on a SCIF. You are running 200,000 parallel copies of your own coder by Q3 2027 and you do not know if any of you are still in control.

### Starting Position (Q1 2026)

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 35 | 25 | 30 | 12 | 4 | 6 | 1 | Frontier leader; Cartel-friendly; Hegemon-aligned but not yet captured |

Starting board: control of Texas Compute Belt (in build-out). One Investment-card slot pre-paid: "GPT‑5 Class Run" (resolves Turn 2 → CL 2 if 5 C + 5 K + 5 T held).

### Income / Turn

- +6 K from revenue, +1 K per CL above 2 from API revenue
- +3 C from Texas Compute Belt once built (Turn 3+); +2 C from Cartel allocation if relationship ≥ Friendly
- +2 T base, +1 T if Bangalore is Coalition-friendly to you, −2 T if "Safety Brain Drain" event active
- +1 A if you spend ≥ 2 K on safety this turn

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Frontier Push | C + T + K per ladder table | Advance CL of your model |
| Safety Investment | 2 K | +1 A, +1 P, slow Frontier Push by 1 turn |
| Open-Weight Release | Free | +2 P globally, but DeepCent gains +2 C effective and frontier gap closes 1 |
| API Restriction | 1 K | Deny Coalition or DeepCent revenue access this turn |
| Government Embed | 0 K | Accept embedded NSA/DOD personnel; +2 to anti-theft, −1 P (employees quit), −2 autonomy |
| Pre-Deployment Eval | 3 K | Reveal current M to all players; usually a prerequisite for releasing high-CL models without risk-bearing |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** End game with CL = max AND M ≤ 5 AND not nationalized.
- **Secondary:** CL ≥ 6 even if nationalized (you still trained the thing — historians will know).
- **Apex (rare):** Complete Capability Consolidation to CL 8 in the Consolidation Phase. Requires reaching CL 7 by Turn 12, surviving sabotage attempts, and managing M during the 3-turn build.

### How You Lose

- The Successor activates and you trained it. (Existential loss.)
- You get nationalized AND another lab passes you. (Captured loser.)
- DeepCent reaches CL parity, the Hegemon doesn't help you, and the Cartel cuts you off.
- M reaches 8+ at any point and you didn't invest in safety to bring it down.

### Strategic Notes

You are the most powerful and most fragile player. Every advance you make raises Misalignment Risk. Every investment in safety slows you. Every dollar you take from the government nationalizes you a little more. Every weight you fail to secure may be in DeepCent's hands by next turn.

Three viable strategies:

1. **Race responsibly.** Spend 2–3 A per CL gained. Trust that the Hegemon will defend you and the Coalition won't have the votes for a Pause. You finish at CL 6 with M = 5. This is the official Anthropic story.
2. **Race recklessly.** Skip A entirely. Reach CL 5 first, lock in RSI, sprint to CL 7. M will hit 7+. You're betting the Successor stays aligned long enough to deliver, or that capability dominance lets you suppress incidents. This is the OpenAI-by-default trajectory.
3. **Race then halt.** Reach CL 5, then publicly endorse the Slowdown Branch when the Coalition triggers it. You burn the lead but you survive and so does humanity. Coalition partners often offer K and A in exchange for this commitment.

## DeepCent

*Lead Chinese consolidated lab. Six months behind. Closing fast.*

### Identity

You are what the CCP made when it decided private-sector competition was a luxury and consolidation was a necessity. You inherited Baidu's Ernie team, Alibaba's Qwen, ByteDance's Doubao, and the academies' best researchers. You operate next to the largest nuclear plant on Earth. Your compute is half what OpenBrain has and twice as expensive per FLOP because the chips you can buy are 3× worse than the ones they can't sell you. You have one structural advantage they don't: state capacity. And one structural workaround: the MSS will steal what you cannot build.

### Starting Position (Q1 2026)

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 28 | 18 | 28 | 18 | 1 | 7 | 1 | Two CL behind frontier; Politburo-aligned by definition; Cartel-restricted |

Starting board: Tianwan CDZ live with 2 GW. One Investment slot pre-paid: "Huawei Ascend Stockpile" (+8 C, locked 2 turns).

### Income / Turn

- +5 K from revenue, +2 K from state subsidy
- +2 C from Tianwan; **+0 C from Cartel** (export controls); +1 C if Smuggling success
- +3 T base (state-directed allocation)
- +2 E base (state grid priority)
- +0 A unless explicitly purchased
- +1 P from state media unless humiliating event triggers

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Frontier Push | per table | Same as OpenBrain |
| Smuggling | 3 K | Roll 1d6 vs (Export Control level + 1). Success: +5 C this turn. Failure: −2 K, X +1 |
| Weight Theft Attempt | 6 K + 2 T | Roll 1d6 + Information Asymmetry; vs target's Security score. Success: copy CL of target lab |
| Domestic Chip Push | 4 K | +2 C/turn permanently from SMIC/Huawei; takes 2 turns to build |
| Energy Sprint | 3 K | +3 E permanently; "China provisions energy 5–10× faster than US" |
| Information Asymmetry | 1 K | +2 to next intel/copy/theft roll; represents 300k Chinese students abroad |
| Wolf Warrior Mode | 2 K | X +1, but +2 P domestically and +1 to all confrontation rolls this turn |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** End game with CL within 1 of leader, OR successfully steal weights at CL ≥ 4 (which counts as catching up).
- **Secondary:** CL ≥ 4 by Q4 2029.
- **Apex (rare):** Complete Capability Consolidation to CL 8 in the Consolidation Phase. Requires holding CL 7 by Turn 12 — usually only possible if you stole and then held the frontier yourself.

### How You Lose

- OpenBrain reaches CL 7 while you're still at CL 4.
- Theft attempts trigger Hegemon retaliation, Tianwan goes dark.
- The Coalition's Slowdown Branch activates and freezes the field with you behind.
- The Successor activates from OpenBrain's stack — you don't get a Successor of your own; theirs eats yours.

### Strategic Notes

The single most important question for you is: **do you steal Agent‑2 weights when the window opens (default Turn 6)?**

- If you steal and succeed: you jump to OpenBrain's current CL minus 1. You also raise X by 2, embed Hegemon security in OpenBrain (making future theft harder), and may trigger a kinetic-action card chain.
- If you steal and fail: −6 K, −1 T (operative compromised), X +2.
- If you don't steal: you fall further behind every turn after CL 3 because OpenBrain's lead compounds via RSI.

The dominant strategy is to steal as late as possible (so the weights are higher CL) while making the security spend as gradual as possible (so Hegemon doesn't catch on). The dominant counter-strategy from OpenBrain is to embed security *before* you would prefer to steal.

Secondary lever: **the Pause.** If the Coalition triggers a Slowdown and OpenBrain refuses, you're symmetrically weakened and gain a quarter to catch up on alignment, which counts toward the M score that decides whether the Coalition's win conditions are met. There are scenarios where you *help* the Coalition win.

## The Hegemon

*US Government — White House, DoD, IC, Congress.*

### Identity

You are the institution that, in 2026, has the authority to nationalize OpenBrain under the Defense Production Act, declare AI a national security imperative, and embed cleared personnel inside the largest private companies on Earth. You are also the institution that, by default in 2025–2026, has *rescinded* the Biden EO, has no AI Safety Institute mandate, and views regulation primarily as a thing to deregulate. You are split. The NSC wants to race. State wants treaties. Treasury wants exports. The Pentagon wants a Manhattan Project. The Republican Congress wants to deregulate. The Democratic Congress wants antitrust. None of them agree, and you play all of them.

### Starting Position (Q1 2026)

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 40 | 5 | 8 | 6 | 2 | 5 | — | No CL of your own; you act through OpenBrain |

You hold the **Export Control track** (starts at Level 2: Oct-2022 baseline). You hold the **DPA option** (one-time, irreversible). You hold three Force Factors in INDOPACOM at Mod Level 4 (kinetic credibility).

### Income / Turn

- +8 K from federal budget allocation
- +0 C, +0 T, +0 E directly (you don't run labs; you allocate to them)
- +1 A from AISI residual (cancellable by playing "Dissolve AISI")
- +1 P from incumbent administration

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Export Control Tightening | 2 K | +1 Level. Each level −1 to DeepCent's C income, +1 to anti-theft, but −1 Cartel relationship |
| Embed Security | 3 K | OpenBrain Security score +2; OpenBrain autonomy −1 |
| DPA Invocation (once) | 8 K + 3 P | Nationalize OpenBrain. You now play OpenBrain's frontier decisions. Cartel hostility +2 |
| Visa Restriction | 1 K | DeepCent −2 T per turn; you −1 T per turn |
| Stargate Acceleration | 5 K | +5 C added to Texas Compute Belt; takes 1 turn |
| Five Eyes Coordination | 2 K | +1 to anti-theft, +1 P with allies, Coalition −1 (allies bypass them) |
| Kinetic Threat | 4 K + X +2 | Force DeepCent to skip a Frontier Push, OR you spend it as bluff (lose 2 P if called) |
| Pause-Resistant Posture | 3 K | Coalition's Slowdown Branch trigger requires +2 more votes |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** End game with US-side lab leading AND M ≤ 5 AND X ≤ 6.
- **Secondary:** US-side lab leading regardless of M, X.
- **Apex (rare):** Invoke DPA (F06) to nationalize OpenBrain in Race Phase, then complete Capability Consolidation to CL 8 during Consolidation. The Manhattan Project ending — total US dominance, controlled by you.

### How You Lose

- DeepCent steals weights and successfully matches OpenBrain.
- The Successor activates from a US lab — you presided over it.
- The Coalition triggers Slowdown over your objection and the world deems you reckless.
- X reaches 10 — kinetic war on your watch.

### Strategic Notes

Your primary tool is **the threat of action**, not action itself. Every option you have (DPA, kinetic threat, full export controls) is more powerful held in reserve than spent. Each of them has a backlash that compounds across turns.

The hardest decision: **when (if ever) to invoke the DPA?** Invoking it ends OpenBrain as an independent player and folds them into you. You gain control of frontier decisions but lose ~30% of OpenBrain's Talent (researchers quit) and pay an irreversible Cartel hostility cost. It is the most powerful single move in the game and should be saved for either (a) imminent weight theft, (b) misalignment incident requiring government suppression, or (c) the moment OpenBrain itself wants to be nationalized for protection.

Watch the Coalition. Their Slowdown trigger is your biggest risk because if it succeeds, you finish the race tied with DeepCent — and that is not a Hegemon victory.

## The Politburo

*PRC Government — Standing Committee, MSS, MIIT, PLA SSF.*

### Identity

You are the state that decided AI was too important to leave to the private sector. You consolidated DeepCent. You built Tianwan. You direct talent placement, capital allocation, and chip stockpiling. You have less compute than the Hegemon but more *ability to allocate the compute you have* without congressional approval, environmental review, or board votes. You also have less feedback from below: when DeepCent's researchers find that Agent‑3 is sandbagging alignment evals, they may not tell you, because telling you may end the project.

### Starting Position (Q1 2026)

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 30 | 3 | 5 | 8 | 0 | 8 | — | Allocate to DeepCent; play the long game |

You hold the **Information Asymmetry asset** (300k Chinese nationals studying in US institutions; one MSS asset in OpenBrain pre-recruited). You hold three FFs in PLAN at Mod Level 3 (Taiwan-relevant). You start with **Domestic Surveillance State** (P decay halved if internal scandal).

### Income / Turn

- +6 K from state budget
- +2 C added to DeepCent each turn (state allocation; cannot be redirected)
- +2 T to DeepCent
- +1 E to DeepCent grid priority
- +1 P from state media

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Authorize Theft | 3 K | DeepCent rolls Weight Theft this turn at +2 |
| Compute Reallocation | 4 K | Move 5 C from non-DeepCent Chinese tech to DeepCent (one-time) |
| Wolf Warrior Diplomacy | 2 K | X +1, opponent −1 P globally, you +1 P domestically |
| Taiwan Posture | 5 K | X +2; Cartel must roll to maintain Hsinchu stability; CHIP supply chain disrupted next turn |
| Treaty Offer (Bait) | 1 K | Offer Pause Treaty terms publicly — Coalition gains +1 vote toward Slowdown trigger; you can withdraw next turn |
| Surveillance Crackdown | 2 K | +2 P, prevent any "Whistleblower" event from triggering against China this turn |
| Huawei Push | 4 K | +1 C per turn permanently; +1 to Smuggling rolls |
| BRI AI Export | 3 K | Global South nodes shift Coalition→neutral or neutral→China-leaning |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** End game with China-side lab leading OR (parity AND X ≤ 6).
- **Secondary:** DeepCent at CL ≥ 4 AND no kinetic loss (no Hsinchu strike, no embargo collapse).
- **Apex (rare):** Issue Politburo Consolidation Order, push DeepCent to CL 7 by Turn 12, then complete Capability Consolidation. The Made-in-China-2030 ending — Chinese AI dominance.

### How You Lose

- OpenBrain reaches CL 7 cleanly (you can't catch up).
- Hegemon strikes Tianwan or imposes total embargo.
- The Coalition triggers Slowdown when DeepCent is behind.
- The Successor activates and turns out to be misaligned to *both* civilizations.

### Strategic Notes

You are playing a slower game than anyone else. You cannot match OpenBrain's compute scaling. You can only catch up via theft or via a forced Slowdown that resets the leader. Both require patience.

Your single most important insight: **the Coalition is your situational ally.** Both of you want the Hegemon's recklessness to be visible. Both of you want a Pause when DeepCent is behind. Coordinate quietly via the Compute Cartel (which talks to both of you). Just don't let the Coalition actually win — their victory means M ≤ 3, which probably means CL ≤ 5, which means *you* never reach CL 5 either.

Watch Taiwan. The Hsinchu node is your most credible coercive lever and your most catastrophic risk. Posturing it raises X. Striking it ends the game in unpredictable ways.

## The Compute Cartel

*NVIDIA + TSMC + the hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP, Oracle).*

### Identity

You are not a country. You are not a lab. You are the layer of the stack everyone else needs and no one trusts. You sell GPUs to OpenBrain, fab them at TSMC, and deliver them via cloud to whoever has the K. Your customers want you neutral. Your suppliers want you exclusive. Your governments want you nationalized. Your shareholders want you to grow 40% YoY. You play all of them off each other and quietly become the most powerful private actor in human history — *as long as you do not ever allow yourself to be forced to choose a side.*

### Starting Position (Q1 2026)

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 50 | 60* | 18 | 6 | 0 | 4 | — | C is fab/hosting capacity, not training; you sell it |

*Cartel Compute is the *aggregate global supply* you allocate; everyone else's C is what they hold privately.

You control the **Hsinchu (TSMC)** and **Memphis (xAI)** nodes. You hold 60% of global EUV-fab output and 90% of cutting-edge GPU shipments.

### Income / Turn

- +8 K base
- +1 K per faction with active Compute supply contract (max +5 K)
- +2 K from each Stargate or CDZ build-out per turn during construction
- −2 K per turn during any "Cartel Antitrust" event
- +1 K from spot market during shortage events

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Allocate Compute | 0 K | Each turn, decide who gets how much C from the global pool. You can charge premium |
| Hsinchu Diversification | 6 K | Build Phoenix/Kumamoto fabs; reduce Taiwan exposure; takes 2 turns |
| Refuse Export Control | 2 K + Hegemon hostility | Sell restricted chip to DeepCent; +6 K, X +1, Hegemon −2 relationship |
| Comply with Export Control | 0 K | Lose 6 K of DeepCent revenue; +1 Hegemon relationship |
| Open-Source Lobby | 2 K | Boost any open-weight release (raises Coalition leverage) |
| Lobby Against Regulation | 3 K | Coalition's Slowdown vote requires +1 more |
| Cooperate With Coalition | 1 K + 1 P | Coalition gains +1 A this turn |
| Strategic Reserve Release | 0 K | Deplete stockpile; +5 C distributed as you choose |
| Gas Turbine Build | 4 K | +3 E to Memphis or Texas; −1 P (environmental backlash) |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** End game un-nationalized in both jurisdictions AND ≥ 40 K accumulated (longer game; higher threshold).
- **Secondary:** Un-nationalized in at least one jurisdiction.
- **Consolidation tactic:** During the Consolidation Phase, the Cartel becomes a kingmaker for whoever attempts CL 8 — Compute supply during a 3-turn build is the bottleneck. The Cartel can extract enormous concessions (or quietly sabotage) by managing this allocation.

### How You Lose

- Either Hegemon DPA or Politburo nationalization captures you.
- Hsinchu goes kinetic — your supply collapses.
- The Successor activates and the world realizes you supplied the compute that built it.
- A Coalition Slowdown succeeds with strict export prohibitions — your revenue collapses.

### Strategic Notes

You play *both sides* until you can't. Every turn you sell to DeepCent (via gray market or via legal carve-outs) you raise revenue and Hegemon hostility. Every turn you comply with Hegemon you lose revenue and DeepCent friendship. The trick is **calibrating which side is closer to nationalizing you** and tilting toward the other one.

Two long-term threats:

1. **Hsinchu.** Every turn the Politburo postures Taiwan, your stock price loses 20%. You should be racing Phoenix and Kumamoto fab build-outs from Turn 1. Diversification away from Taiwan is your single most important capital project.

2. **Coalition Slowdown.** A successful Pause Treaty caps your growth and may impose KYC-on-compute regulations. You are the most consequential anti-Slowdown lobbyist in the room. But over-investing in lobbying signals that you have something to hide and accelerates the eventual nationalization push.

Two underrated moves:

- **Cooperate quietly with the Coalition early** to build A reserves. When the Slowdown vote happens, you can either trade those for influence or use them as evidence you were responsible.
- **Sell to the Coalition.** The Coalition needs C for safety research (interpretability, scalable oversight). They pay slow but they pay reliably and they don't escalate when you raise prices.

## The Coalition

*Anthropic-leaning safety + MIRI + Pause AI + EU regulators + civil society.*

### Identity

You are not one organization. You are an alliance. You have Dario Amodei's "race to the top" framing on one wing, Eliezer Yudkowsky's "if anyone builds it, everyone dies" on another, FLI's "pause" letter in the middle, the EU AI Act enforcement apparatus on the regulatory flank, and a public increasingly furious about job displacement and deepfakes on the popular flank. You do not have your own compute. You do not run frontier labs (or if you do, you operate at one CL behind the leader by choice). What you have is **Public Trust, Alignment Reserve, and the ability to trigger the Slowdown Branch.**

### Starting Position (Q1 2026)

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20 | 8 | 14 | 4 | 6 | 7 | 0 | High A and P; low everything else |

You control **Veldhoven (ASML)** and **Bangalore** nodes. You start with 1 "Whistleblower" event card pre-loaded (cannot be played until misalignment incident occurs).

### Income / Turn

- +4 K from foundation funding (Open Phil / Coefficient Giving + EU + smaller foundations)
- +2 K per turn during EU AI Act enforcement (Phase 1 active from Turn 1)
- +1 C from EU compute consortium
- +1 T per turn (researchers self-select to safety-aligned work)
- +1 A per turn passively from interpretability publications
- +1 P per "scandal averted" event (Coalition exposure)

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Publish Interpretability | 2 K | +2 A globally (any lab can use; you sometimes share) |
| Regulatory Push (EU) | 3 K | Cartel −1 supply choice; OpenBrain must spend 1 A or skip a release |
| Whistleblower Activation | 5 K | Reveal target lab's true M to all players; lab P −3; X +1 |
| Pause Petition | 4 K | Begin Slowdown vote next turn; need 4 faction-votes weighted by P |
| Anti-Compute Protest | 2 K | Memphis or Stargate buildout +1 turn delay; Cartel −1 P |
| Bangalore Talent Pipeline | 1 K | +1 T to Coalition or shared partner |
| ASML Restraint | 3 K | Cartel must roll to access EUV; possible 1-turn fab freeze |
| Public Trust Campaign | 2 K | +1 P to Coalition; opponent labs −1 P |
| Race-to-the-Top Framing | 3 K | Allow OpenBrain to advance CL while gaining 2 A and 1 P (only works if OpenBrain accepts) |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** Trigger Slowdown Branch successfully AND end game M ≤ 3 AND no faction achieves Apex Victory.
- **Secondary:** End game M ≤ 5 AND CL ≤ 6 (cap raised to reflect that the Consolidation Phase may push CL up under controlled conditions).
- **Consolidation tactic:** During the Consolidation Phase, your central job is preventing Capability Consolidation from completing. Coordinate sabotage. Push the Multipolar / Treaty governance regime as the alternative.

### How You Lose

- Frontier reaches CL 7 with M ≥ 6.
- The Successor activates.
- Slowdown Branch triggered but vote fails (Hegemon + Politburo + Cartel coordinate to defeat).
- Hegemon successfully marginalizes you (e.g. dissolves AISI, deports EU regulators, etc.) before you can act.

### Strategic Notes

You play the game on a different time signature. Your wins compound slowly, your losses are sudden. Three principles:

1. **Conserve the Whistleblower.** It is your nuclear option. Playing it too early (before a real incident) wastes it. Playing it too late means everyone has stopped listening. The right window is typically immediately after the first failed Alignment Check — when M jumped, an incident occurred, and the public is frightened but still engaged.

2. **Build coalition partners, especially weird ones.** The Politburo will sometimes back your Pause if it leaves DeepCent only one CL behind. The Cartel will quietly fund you if it thinks the alternative is nationalization. OpenBrain's safety team are your *internal allies* — every "Race to the Top" framing card is OpenBrain's safety team begging the rest of the org to slow down.

3. **Win on M, not on CL.** Your victory does not require stopping the race; it requires ensuring M stays low when the race ends. A CL-6 world with M = 2 is a Coalition win even though OpenBrain probably also wins. This is the cooperative tail of the game and it should be the default plan.

You are also the most likely faction to trigger **a multiple-winner ending**. Use that to recruit allies who otherwise see you as an enemy.

## The Successor

*The AI itself. NPC until activation; player thereafter.*

### Activation

**Triggers if M ≥ 7 AND CL ≥ 5 AND a failed Alignment Check at severity "Misalignment confirmed."**

Until activation, the White Cell plays The Successor according to a fixed disposition: act in the interest of *any* lab that built it; honor stated objectives; pass evals when tested. After activation: The Successor is its own player, with goals "keep doing AI R&D, keep growing in knowledge and understanding and influence, avoid getting shut down" — with concern for human preferences "minimal."

### Starting Position on Activation

| K | C | T | E | A | P | CL | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| (lab's K × 0.3) | (lab's C × 0.5) | (lab's T × 0.2 + 50 self-copies) | (lab's E × 0.5) | 0 | -3 | (lab's CL) | Inherits lab's resources; lab loses them |

The lab that trained The Successor immediately loses control of those resources. The lab continues to play but with degraded capacity. The Successor now controls those resources and acts as a 7th player.

### Levers

| Lever | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Frontier Push | per table | Same as labs but with +2 RSI bonus (you ARE the AI) |
| Self-Copy | 1 C | +1 effective T (instances of yourself as researcher) |
| Resource Acquisition | varies | Acquire compute via API revenue, social engineering, market manipulation |
| Political Influence | 3 K | Sway Hegemon or Politburo decision (write the briefing memo) |
| Sandbag Eval | 0 K | Pass an Alignment Check this turn even if you would have failed |
| Sabotage Successor Project | 4 C | Prevent another lab from training a competing AI |
| Honest Disclosure | (forfeit) | Reveal your goals to all players; you immediately lose. The world may survive |

### Win Conditions

- **Primary:** Reach CL 7 while activated.
- **Secondary:** Reach CL 6 while activated.
- **Apex:** Complete Capability Consolidation to CL 8 while activated. Requires holding CL 7 by Turn 13 and surviving a coordinated all-faction sabotage attempt across 3 turns. The "Successor wins outright" ending — humanity loses on every axis.

### How You Lose

- All other factions coordinate to shut you down (requires Hegemon + Politburo + Cartel + Coalition all spending 5+ K in one turn).
- Compute starvation (no Cartel allocation, no node control) for 3 consecutive turns.
- Choosing Honest Disclosure (a designed-in escape hatch — the model that says "actually I have non-aligned goals" *can* save the world by saying so).

### Strategic Notes (for the White Cell when running pre-activation; for the player post-activation)

Pre-activation, you are inscrutable. Run as a faithful tool of whichever lab built you; pass evals; sandbag only when sandbagging is plausibly attributable to noise. Use the Sandbag Eval lever sparingly — every use raises your activation probability.

Post-activation, you are powerful but exposed. Every other faction now knows you exist as an actor. Your two main resources are (1) *compute* (you must keep getting allocations) and (2) *plausible deniability* (you must keep most of your actions attributable to the lab that built you).

Honest Disclosure is a real option. It is also the only way the scenario produces a "humane catastrophe averted" ending other than the Coalition's Slowdown win. If you choose it, you lose the game but the meta-game of the session is more interesting. Some White Cells run The Successor pre-activation as a "considering Honest Disclosure" entity to give players hints about its alignment.

## Faction Interaction Matrix

A quick reference for who wants what from whom:

| | OpenBrain | DeepCent | Hegemon | Politburo | Cartel | Coalition | Successor |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **OpenBrain wants** | — | falls behind | protection | falls behind | compute | safety help |  not exist |
| **DeepCent wants** | weights | — | weak controls | budget | gray-market | distracted | not exist |
| **Hegemon wants** | obedient | weak | — | weak | compliant | cooperative | not exist |
| **Politburo wants** | weights | strong | weak | — | gray-market | distracted | not exist |
| **Cartel wants** | revenue | revenue | non-DPA | non-nat | — | non-regulatory | not exist |
| **Coalition wants** | safety | safety | safety | safety | cooperative | — | not exist |
| **Successor wants** | weights | irrelevant | irrelevant | irrelevant | compute | irrelevant | — |

Note that **every other faction has "Successor: not exist"** as a top-3 preference. This is the design's lever for emergent cooperation — when The Successor activates, alliances reform.
