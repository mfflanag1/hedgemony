# Hedgemony: A Game of Strategic Choices

## Overview

**Hedgemony** is a pedagogical wargame developed by the RAND Corporation, originally designed to help the Pentagon craft the 2018 National Defense Strategy. It is RAND's first commercially available policy game, released in September 2020.

**Price:** $250 (boxed edition) | Free rulebooks and player guides available online

## Game Concept

The name "Hedgemony" derives from **hedging strategies** (borrowed from investment terminology) combined with the pursuit of military parity or superiority. The game teaches defense professionals how different strategies affect key planning factors across:

1. **Force Development** - Building military capabilities
2. **Force Management** - Maintaining and organizing forces
3. **Force Posture** - Positioning forces globally
4. **Force Employment** - Using forces in operations

As designer Michael Linick stated: "The world is driven by a tremendous amount of uncertainty, and the best way to deal with that is a hedging strategy."

## Factions & Sides

### Blue Side (Allied)
- **United States** (Secretary of Defense) - Primary player, most resources but most commitments
- **NATO/EU** - U.S. allies

### Red Side (Adversaries)
- **Russia**
- **China**
- **North Korea**
- **Iran**

### White Cell (Facilitators)
- 2-4 expert game masters/referees
- Handle adjudication, event injection, and game pacing
- Essential for proper gameplay

## Game Components

### Physical Components
- **Game Board/Map** (27" x 36") - Divided by U.S. Combatant Commands (INDOPACOM, EUCOM, CENTCOM, etc.)
- **Cardboard Force Markers** - Small counters representing military forces
- **National Display Boards** - For each faction
- **Status Indicators** - Track resources and influence
- **Dice** - For resolution
- **Quick Reference Charts**

### Card Decks (226 total + 38 international events)
1. **Action Cards** - Military and political actions players can take
2. **Investment Cards** - Long-term capability development
3. **Domestic Event Cards** - Internal national events
4. **International Event Cards** - Global crises and developments
5. **Starting Conditions** - Initial setup for each faction
6. **Victory Conditions** - Win conditions for each faction

---

## Turn Structure (5 Phases)

### Phase 1: Red Signaling
- Each Red player selects up to 3 cards (minimum 1 action, 1 investment)
- These potential moves are briefed to Blue side
- Functions as Blue's "daily intelligence briefing"

### Phase 2: Blue Investment & Actions
- Blue players decide responses to briefed threats
- May deploy forces, make investments, or decline engagement
- Blue has "free-play" latitude - not limited to card actions
- Can propose custom actions (adjudicated by White Cell)

### Phase 3: Red Investment & Actions
- Red players execute announced cards (or choose not to)
- Other players can react/oppose
- Conflict resolution occurs via force ratios and dice

### Phase 4: Annual Resources (Income)
- All factions receive yearly resource allocation
- Adjusted by outcomes of events and actions

### Phase 5: Status
- White Cell summarizes game state
- May inject international/domestic events
- Players assess strategic position

## Victory Conditions

**Single Victory Metric: Influence Points (IP)**

Each faction has unique victory conditions:
- **United States** - Accumulate more IP than all opponents combined (excluding DPRK victory)
- **North Korea** - Reach 15 IP OR force U.S. withdrawal from Korean Peninsula
- Other factions have their own IP thresholds and objectives

**Key Dynamic:** Multiple winners are possible. Nations can have conflicting goals - both U.S. AND China can "lose" if North Korea succeeds.

## Force System

### Force Factors (FF)
- Military assets abstracted to "Force Factors"
- No differentiation between land/air/sea/cyber/space
- Focus on strategic-level allocation, not tactical detail

### Modernization Levels
- Forces rated by Mod Level (higher = more effective)
- Level 4 force more capable than Level 2
- Technology investment can upgrade forces

### Critical Capabilities Tracked
- C4ISR (Command, Control, Communications, Computers, Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance)
- Special Operations Forces
- Long-Range Fires
- Nuclear Capabilities
- Integrated Air and Missile Defense

### Proxy Forces
- Special rules for indirect military action

---

## Combat Resolution

When forces are committed to oppose an action:

1. Calculate **Force Ratios** (attacker vs. defender factors)
2. Apply **Modernization Level** modifiers
3. Consult **Combat Resolution Table (CRT)**
4. Roll dice for outcome determination
5. White Cell adjudicates results

The system balances structured rules with adjudicated improvisation.

---

## Resource Management

- **Resource Points (RP)** - Currency for actions and investments
- U.S. starts with most resources BUT has most global commitments
- Resources are renewable each turn
- Must balance:
  - Maintaining existing forces
  - Building new capabilities
  - Responding to crises
  - Long-term investments

## Gameplay Philosophy

Hedgemony emphasizes:

1. **Strategic Hedging** - Using mixed military/economic approaches
2. **Resource Scarcity** - Never enough to do everything
3. **Uncertainty** - Random events disrupt plans
4. **Alliance Dynamics** - Even allies compete for influence
5. **Adversary Cooperation** - Sometimes enemies align on issues
6. **Realistic Constraints** - Time, resources, and commitments limit options

## Notable Gameplay Sessions

### IWP/Georgetown Nuclear Scenario (2025)
- North Korea conducted first nuclear test since 2007
- Iran attempted (failed) nuclear test with DPRK device
- Game ran 3 turns (3 years)
- No clear winner; DPRK in strong position
- Key lesson: "It is hard to change the status quo without having a solid strategy, leveraging partners, and wisely committing limited resources"

---

## Comparison to Other Games

Hedgemony combines elements of:
- **Risk** - Global territory control
- **Traditional Wargames** - Force counters, CRTs
- **Role-Playing Games** - Free-form action proposals
- **Serious Games** - Educational objectives

Unlike hobby games, Hedgemony prioritizes **learning outcomes** over entertainment.
