## How combat factors are derived (rulebook ch. 10, p.42)

Each side's committed Force Factors are converted to **Combat Factors (CF)** using
the force counts, their **Mod Levels**, and relevant Critical Capabilities; U.S. CF is
additionally affected by **Readiness**. The two sides' CFs give a **ratio / advantage**
that selects a column on the resolution table.

## Combat Resolution Table A (CRT A) — combat interactions

- **Rows:** D10 result (plus modifiers).
- **Columns:** the attacker/defender CF advantage band (e.g. strong Red advantage …
  even … strong Blue advantage).
- **Cells:** an outcome — typically one of `RED Major Gain`, `RED Minor Gain`,
  `Status Quo`, `BLUE Minor Gain`, `BLUE Major Gain` (with associated force losses /
  pinning and IP swings adjudicated by the White Cell).

## Resolution Table B (RT B) — noncombat interactions

- Same row/column shape as CRT A, used for gray-zone, economic, diplomatic, and other
  **noncombat** actions (e.g. sample card PRC-01 "use RT B").
- Cells yield the same outcome bands (Red/Blue major/minor gain, status quo); the
  acting card's own outcome table maps those bands to IP/RP changes.

## MVP note

The engine does **not** auto-resolve these tables (the White Cell rolls and applies
outcomes, as in the real game). The console provides a D10 roller and displays the
table for reference.
